Dutch Baby Pancake (German Pancake)

Published Jan 03, 2017 — Makes 4 servings — ⏱ 30 minutes

From legendary Henry Thiele’s Restaurant (closed 1990) on West Burnside “German Pancake” adapted/refined from James Beard

## Ingredients

3 eggs, at room temperature

¾ cup non-fat half-and-half, at room temperature

2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted (divided)

1 tablespoon sugar

½ teaspoon salt

2 teaspoons lemon juice

¾ cup all-purpose flour

Powdered sugar, for dusting

## Instructions

Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Place a 10-inch cast-iron skillet in the oven while it heats.

In a mixing bowl, beat together eggs, half-and-half, 1 tablespoon melted butter, sugar, salt, and lemon juice.

Add flour and whisk until incorporated.

Remove hot skillet from oven and add the remaining 1 tablespoon butter, spreading it across the bottom and sides (a silicone pastry brush works well).

Pour the batter into the pan from the center, letting it push the butter toward the edges.

Return the skillet to the oven and bake 20–25 minutes, until puffed in the center and golden brown.

Dust with powdered sugar before slicing into wedges.

Optional (Beard-Thiele style): Brush with melted butter and lemon juice, sprinkle with granulated sugar, then dust with powdered sugar before serving.
